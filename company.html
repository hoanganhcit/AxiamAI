The Journey Behind Axiam

From costly account takeovers to passwordless, live-person authentication. Axiam secures identities with privacy-first facial sign‑in.

Our Story

For more than fifteen years as a Chief Technology Officer, Henry Quach saw firsthand the immense cost of user account takeovers. Each breach meant more than stolen data—it led to days of recovery, lost productivity, shaken client confidence, and in some cases millions of dollars in damage. Even with the strongest defenses—complex passwords, multi-factor authentication, and hardware tokens—attackers found ways in. Phishing fooled employees, SIM swapping bypassed text-based MFA, and malware silently harvested codes. Every incident reinforced a painful reality: traditional security leaves organizations vulnerable and expensive to repair.

The breaking point came when a high-value corporate account was hijacked despite multiple security layers. Stolen credentials and bypassed MFA opened the door, resulting in operational disruption and lasting reputational harm. Henry recognized the core weakness—any system built on shared secrets can be stolen, guessed, or phished.

Determined to eliminate this costly threat, Henry pioneered Facial Sign-In as a Service. Unlike passwords or codes, a face cannot be stolen, written down, or intercepted. With biometric verification, liveness detection, and end-to-end encrypted templates, the system shuts down the attack vectors that drive account takeover. There are no passwords to phish, no codes to intercept, and no tokens to misplace. Hackers cannot replicate a live, breathing identity in real time.

Today, Henry champions facial sign-in as more than a convenience—it is a critical defense against one of the most expensive and pervasive cyber threats of our time. By authenticating people through their true identity, organizations gain faster, more private, and enterprise-grade protection. His journey underscores a powerful truth: the path to stopping account takeovers begins by removing the very tools attackers exploit. Facial sign-in is that path.
